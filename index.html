Black Women Rock

For the last few years, people have been paying more and more attention to the black community, and especially the women becoming their most popular faces. Amongst the likes of Michelle Obama, Beyoncé, Rihanna and else, there are other amazing black women which have been household names for a while.
Here are 4 of them who inspired me the most

  • Misty Copeland

    Misty came to light after being promoted as the first African-American principal dancer for the American Ballet Theatre.

    Ever since, she took on being more vocal on issues and challenges dancers of color are facing, wherever they aspire to a professional career or not.

    "My body is very different from most of the dancers I dance with. My hair is different than most I dance with. But I didn't let that stop me. Black girls rock and can be ballerinas."

    Learn more about her story.

  • Gabrielle Union

    Gabrielle's career covers the last 3 decades and keeps on growing steadily.

    She was sadly victim of an assault aged 19 and credited Oprah Winfrey as one of the persons who helped her recover. She has since become an assault survivor activist.

    The controversy surrounding her latest movie "The Birth Of A Nation" provided her a new platform to talk about the consequences and various effects of such assaults.

    "My personal discomfort is nothing compared to being a voice for people who feel absolutely voiceless and powerless."

  • Viola Davis

    Viola entered Hollywood's history by becoming the first African-American woman to win an Emmy Award for her leading role in "How To Get Away With Murder".

    Her story is a true example from rags to riches, as she fought her way up from deep poverty to the gates of Rhodes Island College and Juilliard School. Her acting talents go easily from stage to the front of a camera.

    "I believe that the privilege of a lifetime is being who you are, truly being who you are. And I've spent far too long apologizing for that—my age, my color, my lack of classical beauty—that now at the age of, well, at the age of 46, I'm very proud to be Viola Davis, for whatever it's worth."

  • Laverne Cox

    Laverne Cox first appeared on VH1's program "I Want To Work For Diddy" before producing her own TV show "TRANSform Me" in 2010.

    Now part of the cast of famous "Orange Is The New Black", Laverne is constantly promoting LGBTQ rights and is championing a greater visibility of trans actors and actresses.

    "Each and every one of us has the capacity to be an oppressor. I want to encourage each and everyone of us to interrogate how we might be an oppressor and how we might be able to become liberators for ourselves and for each other."
